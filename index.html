Everyday physics · source closed

Why does metal feel colder than wood in the same room?

Learner’s first explanation Unscored

Metal is naturally colder than wood, so it pulls heat out of your hand.

Question the link

Both materials began at room temperature. What property—not temperature—would make heat leave your hand at different rates?

Repair to reconstruct later

Metal conducts heat away from skin faster, even when the metal and wood begin at the same temperature.

Learner’s later reconstruction Source closed again

Metal feels colder because it conducts heat away from my skin faster, even though the metal and wood begin at the same temperature.

Now evidence The learner rebuilt the connection after spacing. The explanation they read remains context.
